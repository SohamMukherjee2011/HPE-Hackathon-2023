Normal Hacking vs Ethical Hacking

A straight forward difference

What is Hacking?

Hacking refers to the act of using various techniques to gain unauthorized access to a computer system or network. The goal of hacking can vary, but it often involves stealing sensitive information, compromising security measures, or causing damage to the system. There are many types of hacking, including ethical hacking, which is done with the goal of finding and fixing vulnerabilities in a system, and malicious hacking, which is done with the goal of causing harm. Hacking techniques can include exploiting vulnerabilities in software or hardware, guessing passwords, social engineering, and using malware such as viruses or Trojans. It is important to note that hacking without proper authorization is illegal and can result in serious legal consequences. Ethical hacking, on the other hand, is conducted with the permission of the system owner and is intended to improve security.


What is Ethical Hacking?

Ethical hacking, also known as white-hat hacking, is the practice of using hacking techniques for constructive purposes, with the goal of identifying vulnerabilities in a system and helping to improve its security. Ethical hackers, also known as security researchers, are hired by organizations to identify and report on potential security weaknesses in their systems, applications, or networks. The objective of ethical hacking is to simulate the actions of a malicious attacker in order to identify vulnerabilities that could be exploited. By finding these vulnerabilities and reporting them to the system owner, ethical hackers help to improve the overall security of the system and reduce the risk of cyber attacks. Ethical hacking typically involves a range of techniques, including penetration testing, vulnerability scanning, and social engineering. It requires a deep understanding of computer systems, networks, and security protocols, as well as knowledge of the latest hacking techniques and tools.

Know more about Ethical Hacking

why ?

Ethical hacking, also known as "penetration testing", is a process of identifying and exploiting vulnerabilities in computer systems, networks, or applications with the purpose of finding potential security weaknesses before malicious hackers can exploit them. Ethical hacking helps organizations to strengthen their security measures and protect themselves from cyber attacks. By performing ethical hacking, companies can identify weaknesses in their systems and take proactive measures to mitigate those vulnerabilities.

Benifits

Identifying security vulnerabilities: Ethical hacking helps organizations to identify vulnerabilities in their computer systems, networks, and applications. By finding these weaknesses, organizations can take steps to fix them and improve their overall security posture. Preventing cyber attacks: By identifying vulnerabilities before malicious hackers can exploit them, ethical hacking can prevent cyber attacks from occurring. This can save organizations from costly data breaches and reputational damage.

Setup

The first step is to define the scope of the ethical hacking project. This involves identifying the systems, networks, or applications that will be tested, as well as any limitations or constraints on the testing. Obtain permission: It is essential to obtain permission from the organization before conducting ethical hacking. This involves getting written consent and ensuring that all stakeholders are aware of the testing and its potential impact.     Keep your software updated: Make sure that all your software, including operating systems, web browsers, and applications, are up-to-date with the latest security patches and updates.

  • Stage 2

    Use strong passwords: Use strong, unique passwords for all your accounts, and avoid using the same password for multiple accounts. Use a password manager to generate and store your passwords securely.
